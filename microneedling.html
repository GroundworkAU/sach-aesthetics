Microneedling

What it is

Collagen, from the inside out

Microneedling is a minimally invasive treatment designed to boost collagen production and rejuvenate the skin.

A device with fine, sterile needles creates tiny microchannels on the skin's surface. These controlled micro injuries trigger your body's natural healing response, stimulating the production of collagen and elastin, the building blocks for smooth, firm skin.

Results build over a course rather than a single session. Most concerns respond best to three to six treatments spaced four to six weeks apart.

Target areas

Face, neck, décolletage, breasts, back, hips, stomach and scalp.

Skin concerns

Fine lines, wrinkles, enlarged pores, acne, scarring, dark spots and stretch marks.

Hair growth

Scalp microneedling stimulates the follicle and is often paired with a topical growth serum.

Day of treatment

Come with clean skin and no makeup on the treatment area. Numbing cream is applied and left to work for around 30 minutes.

Afterwards

Expect redness similar to mild sunburn for 24 to 48 hours. Keep the area clean, skip the gym and hot showers for a day.

Not suitable if

You are pregnant, taking roaccutane, or have an active breakout, infection or eczema in the treatment area.
